Frequently Asked Questions


What is screening?

Screening is a test done before you have symptoms. The idea of the test is to pick up any issue before the onset of symptoms. Tests done when a person presents with symptoms becomes diagnostic test. Screening tests tells us if there is any issue or abnormality that needs further investigation to confirm or refute an issue.

What does a lump in breast mean?

Lump in the breast or anywhere in the body means increased cell growth. You see the doctor to find out the cause for the lump. Breast lumpds are benign, meaning harmless 80% of the time. But that needs to be conformed by the Breast Specialist. So do get it checked.

How often is Breast self examination needed?

Breast Self Examination is something all women from the early 20s need to do to undetstand the normal feel of their breasts. It is not done to search for lumps. This test is done the week after periods, when the breats are soft and the pre menstrual pain and discomfort is gone. It should be done once in 1-3 months, after periods. if the breasts feel different from the previous examination, visit the Breast Specialist. You can check out how to do Breast Self Examination the right way in our Youtube channel.

When should HPV vaccine be taken?

HPV or Human Papilloma Virus is the main cause of Cervical Cancer. And the HPV Vaccine provides protection against it. The vaccine is recommended in girls 9-14 years of age. If missed in that age range, it can be given till 26 years of age preferably before the onset on sexual activity. The vaccine is given as 2 doses 2 months apart In kids below 15 years and as 3 doses in older kids.

Is cancer genetic?

Cancer is caused due to abnormal cell growth. The signals for this is carriend on genes. So it is genetic. Weather that information is familial or not is the question. 90% cancers are sporadic, meaning they can affect anyone in random, and are not familial or hereditary. Very few cancers carry the hereditary risk on specific genes. Genetic couseling for cancer invloves detailed history about various cancers in family, zeroing on possibility of familial genetic risk, and checking for presence of abnormal gene in participants. Again, carrying abnormal gene does not mean a person will get cancer. it just means an increased risk compared to someone with the gene. Feel free to talk to our specialist and clarify any doubts you may have. We also have a video on genetics and cancer in our Youtube channel.

If i have a lump in my breast, does it mean cancer?

No. 80% of breast lumps are not cancer. Can be due to harmless cysts or fat swellings. Always get it checked with your Breast Specialist
